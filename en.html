Our story

A kitchen that listens to the land.

2019

Racines was born in 2019 from the meeting of two passions: Belgian gastronomy and the plant world. Chef Julien Marchal, trained in several Michelin-starred kitchens, chose to leave the capital and settle in Namur to reconnect with local producers from the Walloon region.

Here, vegetables are not a constraint — they are the starting point. Each dish is built around a seasonal vegetable, mushroom or wild plant, crafted using haute cuisine techniques: fermentation, slow cooking, emulsions. The menu changes entirely every six weeks, dictated by the harvests of partner farms within an 80 km radius.

The Racines dining room, set in a former manor house in the centre of Namur, seats 28 guests in a warm, understated setting — exposed stone, raw wood, dimmed lighting. An open kitchen allows guests to observe the work in real time. The experience is immersive, quiet and sincere.
